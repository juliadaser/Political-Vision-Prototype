About the Project

What if biased algorithms of search engines and news pages are the modern Toxoplasma Parasite - manipulating and altering our access to different political perspectives for their benefit?

Through an on-body-technology headpiece, Political Vision speculates on a potential dangerous relationship between bias of information resources and humans, in which news sources control our access to information in order to alter our political viewpoints, actions and emotions.

The project is originally inspired by the parasitic relationship between cats and mice. Upon contact with toxoplasma parasites through cats, the mouse's brain chemistry is permanently manipulated to make it more susceptible to predation by cats - it alters the mouse’s learning ability, its fear and anxiety response as well as its dopamine production. A similar phenomenon can be observed in humans called the filter-bubble effect, a self-reinforcing pattern present in search engines and political newspapers that reduces human’s creativity, learning, and perspectives.

by Julia Daser 2023
